Fire Damage Restoration

Recovering From Fire Is a Process — We Handle Every Stage

A house fire is one of the most devastating events a family can face. Beyond the visible destruction, hidden hazards — structural damage, toxic soot, carcinogenic smoke residue, and secondary water damage from firefighting — make professional restoration essential, not optional.

ITD Restoration's certified fire damage specialists respond quickly, assess comprehensively, and restore completely. We handle the hard work so you can focus on your family.

What We Handle

Emergency Board-Up & Tarping

Immediate property securing to prevent weather intrusion and vandalism

Water Damage Mitigation

Extraction and drying from firefighting water before mold sets in

Smoke & Soot Removal

Specialized cleaning of all surfaces including HVAC systems

Odor Neutralization

Thermal fogging and hydroxyl generators eliminate smoke odors completely

Structural Repair

Full rebuilding of fire-damaged framing, drywall, roofing, and finishes

Insurance Documentation

Complete photo documentation and scope of work for your adjuster

Our Fire Restoration Process

1

Assessment & Safety Check

We assess structural integrity, identify hidden hazards, and document all damage before any work begins. Your safety — and our team's — is the first priority.

2

Emergency Board-Up & Securing

We immediately board up windows and doors, tarp damaged roofing, and secure the structure against weather, pests, and unauthorized entry.

3

Water Removal & Drying

Firefighting water causes rapid secondary damage. We extract standing water and dry all affected materials before mold has a chance to develop.

4

Smoke, Soot & Odor Remediation

Using HEPA air scrubbers, chemical sponges, and thermal fogging equipment, we remove toxic residues and eliminate smoke odors from every surface and air cavity.

5

Full Structural Restoration

Our licensed contractors rebuild what was damaged — framing, insulation, drywall, flooring, cabinetry, painting, and roofing — returning your home to pre-loss condition.
