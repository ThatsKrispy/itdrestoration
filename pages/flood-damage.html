Flood Damage

Floods Don't Wait for Business Hours — Neither Do We

South Florida ranks among the most flood-prone regions in the United States. Storm surge from hurricanes, intense tropical rainfall, and overwhelmed stormwater systems can push floodwaters into your home with little warning. When that happens, every minute of standing water compounds the damage.

Floodwaters aren't just water — they carry sewage, chemicals, bacteria, and debris that contaminate everything they touch. Category 3 "black water" flooding requires specialized safety protocols that only trained professionals should attempt.

Our Flood Response Process

1

Rapid On-Site Assessment

We classify water category (clean, gray, or black), document all damage, and create a remediation plan — typically within the first hour on site.

2

Content Protection

We move and protect your belongings — furniture, electronics, valuables — to minimize losses from ongoing water exposure.

3

Water Extraction

Truck-mounted and portable extractors remove standing water from all affected areas, including subfloors, crawl spaces, and wall cavities.

4

Disinfection

All flood-affected surfaces are treated with EPA-registered disinfectants to neutralize bacteria, sewage contamination, and pathogens.

5

Structural Drying

Industrial drying systems run 24/7 until all moisture readings reach acceptable levels — verified daily with calibrated meters.

6

Rebuild & Restore

Our licensed contractors handle all necessary repairs — flooring, drywall, cabinetry, paint — restoring your property to pre-flood condition.

Frequently Asked Questions

Standard homeowner's insurance does NOT cover flood damage from external water (storm surge, rising rivers). This requires a separate Flood Insurance policy, typically through the National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP). However, if flooding resulted from an internal event (burst pipe, roof failure), your homeowner's policy may cover it. We help you document and file your claim regardless of coverage type.

Yes. Storm flooding is classified as Category 3 (black water) — the most dangerous category. It contains sewage, chemicals, pesticides, and dangerous pathogens. Do not wade through flood water without protective gear, and do not consume anything that has contacted flood water. Keep children and pets out of flooded areas until they've been professionally decontaminated.

In South Florida's climate, mold can begin developing within 24–48 hours of water exposure. This is why rapid response is critical. The longer standing water sits, the more extensive and expensive the remediation becomes. Calling us immediately after a flood gives us the best chance of preventing secondary mold damage entirely.
