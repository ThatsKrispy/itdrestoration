Mold Remediation

Mold Is More Than an Eyesore — It's a Health Hazard

In South Florida's warm, humid climate, mold can develop within 24–48 hours of water exposure. Once established, it spreads through walls, floors, and HVAC systems — degrading air quality and causing serious respiratory and neurological health effects for your family.

ITD Restoration follows strict IICRC S520 mold remediation standards. We don't just clean the surface — we eliminate the source, contain the spread, and verify clearance before we leave.

South Florida Warning: The combination of high humidity, frequent flooding, and aging housing stock makes Broward, Palm Beach, Miami-Dade, and Martin Counties among the highest-risk areas in the U.S. for mold growth. If you've had water intrusion in the past 48 hours, call us now — don't wait for visible signs.

Signs You May Have a Mold Problem

Visible Discoloration

Black, green, or white spots on walls, ceilings, or grout

Musty Odors

Persistent earthy smell — especially in bathrooms, basements, or after rain

Respiratory Symptoms

Coughing, sneezing, or worsening allergies when inside the property

Recent Water Damage

Any flooding, leaks, or water intrusion in the past 30–90 days

Bubbling or Peeling Paint

Wall surfaces showing moisture-related deterioration

High Indoor Humidity

Condensation on windows or consistently damp-feeling rooms

Our Remediation Process

1

Inspection & Testing

We conduct a thorough visual inspection and, when needed, air sampling and surface testing to identify mold species, extent, and the underlying moisture source driving growth.

2

Containment

Affected areas are sealed with negative air pressure containment barriers to prevent mold spores from spreading to unaffected areas during removal.

3

HEPA Air Filtration

Industrial HEPA air scrubbers run continuously throughout remediation to capture airborne spores before they can resettle.

4

Removal & Disposal

Contaminated materials are removed, bagged, and disposed of following EPA guidelines. Non-porous surfaces are cleaned with EPA-registered antimicrobials.

5

Moisture Correction

We identify and address the moisture source — leaks, poor ventilation, HVAC issues — to ensure mold cannot return after remediation.

6

Post-Remediation Verification

A clearance test confirms spore counts have returned to acceptable levels before we remove containment and return your space to normal.

Frequently Asked Questions

Visible mold growth, musty odors, health symptoms that improve when you leave the building, and any history of water damage are all warning signs. If you're unsure, call us for a professional inspection. We'd rather tell you it's nothing than have you ignore a serious problem.

Bleach kills surface mold on non-porous materials but is ineffective on porous surfaces like drywall, wood, and grout where mold roots (hyphae) penetrate below the surface. Bleach also doesn't address the spores in the air. Professional remediation is required to fully eliminate mold from a structure.

Coverage depends on the cause. Mold resulting from a covered water damage event (burst pipe, storm damage) is typically covered. Mold from a long-term unresolved leak or neglect usually is not. We assist with documentation to support your claim and will advise based on your specific situation.

Small to medium remediation projects typically take 1–5 days. Larger infestations involving multiple rooms or structural materials may take 1–2 weeks. We provide a timeline with our written scope of work before starting.
